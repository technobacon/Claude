DESCRIPTION
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
You know the moment. Twenty minutes into a form, a comment, an application,
an essay — and the page times out, the tab crashes, the site logs you out,
the back button eats it. Everything you typed: gone.

Retype makes that moment impossible.

It quietly records what you type into any text field on any website —
stored only on your computer — and gives it back with one click.

⌨️ AUTOMATIC — install it and forget it. No setup, no account. Retype
backs up your typing as you go.

⏱ THE RESCUE — page wiped your work? Open Retype, hit Insert, and your
text is back in the field. Or copy it and paste anywhere.

🔍 A TIME MACHINE, NOT JUST A FORM SAVER — browse and search everything
you've typed, on any site, from the last month. That comment you wrote
last Tuesday? Two keystrokes away.

🔒 GENUINELY PRIVATE — everything stays in your browser's local storage.
No server. No sync. No telemetry. Password and credit-card fields are
never recorded — the value of a password field is never even read. Pause
any site with one click.

FAQ (full version at the website — see FAQ.md)

Q: How do I know my text won't be leaked?
A: There is nowhere for it to go — Retype has no server, no account, no
sync, no analytics. Verify it yourself: watch the Network tab in DevTools
while you type (zero requests), or disconnect from the internet — Retype
keeps working. The code ships unminified so anyone can read it.

Q: Isn't this a keylogger?
A: A keylogger hides from you and sends keystrokes to someone else. Retype
shows you everything it records, lets you pause or delete it all, and has
no ability to transmit anything. It exists so YOU can recover YOUR work.

Q: What about passwords and cards?
A: Password fields are never even read — the check happens before the
value is touched. Fields that look like card numbers, SSNs, PINs or
one-time codes are refused by a second, independently tested filter.

Q: Why the "read data on all websites" warning?
A: That's Chrome's standard wording for any extension that works on every
site — recording your typing anywhere requires it. What the code actually
does: watch editable fields, save locally, restore on your click.

Q: What happens when I uninstall?
A: Chrome deletes Retype's storage with it. Everything is gone. Export
first if you want to keep your archive.

Install it before you need it. You will need it.
